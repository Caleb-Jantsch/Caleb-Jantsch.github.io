A1: Analysis Question

Does churn vary significantly among customers of differing contract lengths?

A2: Benefits of Analysis to Stakeholders

In this analysis, I will be looking at whether contract length has an impact on customer churn. Understanding what factors affect customer churn is of critical importance to the organization. This information can be used to help predict churn in the future and to create strategies for better customer retention.

A3: Data Used for Analysis

For this analysis, I will be using the churn and contract columns. The churn column contains either "Yes" or "No" indicating whether a customer ended their contract that month. The contract column contains the values "Month-to-month", "Two Year", and "One Year". These values indicate the length of each customer's contract.

B3: Justification for Chosen Technique

For this analysis, I chose to use a chi-square test. The variables I used, churn and contract, are both qualitative variables and contain nominal data. T-test and ANOVA both require quantitative data so they were not suitable for this analysis. Chi-square on the other hand is used for determining the whether there is a significant association between qualitative variables making it the appropriate choice for this analysis.

E1: Results of Hypothesis Test Discussion

The purpose of this analysis was to determine if the observed difference in churn of customers of different contract types was statistically significant. Looking at the table generated for the analysis, we see that customers with monthly contracts have significantly higher churn than customers with one year and two year contracts. The null hypothesis for this analysis is that this observed difference is not statistically significant. The alternative hypothesis would be that the observed difference is significant and that a realtionship exists between the variables. The analysis yielded a p value of 9.11e-157 which is much lower than our alpha of 0.05. Therefore, we can reject the null hypothesis and say that the observed difference between the variables is statistically significant.

E2: Limitations of Analysis

The chi-square analysis can only tell us if the observed difference is statistically significant. It does not establish causation. For this analysis, we can only say that churn and contract type are related. We can not say that a customers contract type will cause them to leave.

Another limitation to chi-square is that it can only be used with categorical variables. It cannot be used with quantitative data.

E3: Recommendations

The next step for this analysis would be to further investigate the relationship between contract type and churn by looking at other variables and how they differ between contract types to establish the cause of the relationship between contract type and churn.
